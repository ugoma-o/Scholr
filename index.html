Why it works

Active recall

Producing answers under pressure is the most effective method of consolidating memory — not re-reading.

The protégé effect

Teaching forces your brain to organise information coherently, surfacing gaps you never knew you had.

Gap mapping

Both modes track where your understanding breaks down in real time — giving you a precise study prescription.

Voice-first

Speak your answers naturally. Verbal articulation strengthens neural encoding more than typing alone.
